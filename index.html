About

About this blog. This portfolio serves as a semester-long record of our learning journey throughout the Ethical Hacking and Penetration Testing course. Each weekly entry documents the concepts, techniques, tools, and security principles covered during class.

This blog is a collective work by Heraisya and Anastasia. Across Weeks 1–13, we explore topics ranging from information gathering and target enumeration to exploitation, privilege escalation, reporting, and final review. The purpose of this portfolio is to document our learning progress and demonstrate our understanding of ethical hacking concepts in an academic environment.

⚠ For educational purposes only. The purpose of this blog is to document and share our academic learning experience. All content is presented for educational purposes only and is based on activities performed within authorized learning environments.

WEEK 01
Introduction and Information Gathering
▾

Week Focus

The topic of introduction and information gathering contributes significantly to the overall penetration testing lifecycle. It introduces practical techniques that allow security professionals to gather information, assess systems, identify weaknesses, and communicate findings effectively. Many specialized tools are used throughout this stage, helping automate tasks while providing valuable insight into the target environment. In addition to the technical aspects, this topic demonstrates the importance of planning, analysis, and decision-making when performing cybersecurity assessments. Organizations benefit from these practices because they can proactively discover vulnerabilities before they are exploited by malicious actors. The knowledge gained from studying this topic supports the development of stronger security strategies and promotes a deeper understanding of defensive cybersecurity practices.

WEEK 01
Introduction and Information Gathering
▾

Week Focus

Introduction and Information Gathering is an important part of the penetration testing process. During this topic, various concepts, tools, and methods are introduced to help security professionals understand how attackers and defenders interact within modern computing environments. The purpose of studying introduction and information gathering is to improve the ability to identify risks, understand system behavior, and strengthen security controls. Several techniques are commonly used in this area, depending on the objectives of the assessment and the environment being examined. Understanding these concepts provides a foundation for performing security assessments in a structured and responsible manner. The topic also highlights the importance of ethical considerations, proper authorization, and professional conduct when handling cybersecurity activities. By applying the principles discussed during this session, organizations can better identify weaknesses, reduce risks, and improve their overall security posture.
